Context

Natural disasters generate solidarity — but also chaos.

Data fragmentation obscures the reality of needs on the ground.

Massive spontaneous donations

Unprecedented goodwill leads to overwhelming influxes of supplies that shelters aren't prepared to process.

Many uncoordinated actors

Government, NGOs, and individual volunteers act simultaneously, leading to duplicated efforts and blind spots.

Information fragmentation

Critical needs are broadcast across WhatsApp groups, social media posts, and disconnected spreadsheets.

Increased vulnerability

Ultimately, this lack of coordination means aid doesn't reach the right people at the right time, worsening the crisis.
